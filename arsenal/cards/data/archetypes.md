# MTG Deck Archetypes

## Primary Strategy Archetypes

### Aggro
**Goal**: Win as fast as possible, usually by attacking with efficient creatures before the opponent can stabilize.
- **Key characteristics**: Low mana curve (1-3 CMC mostly), many creatures, pump spells, burn spells to finish.
- **Win mechanism**: Creature combat damage + direct burn spells to finish.
- **Strengths**: Speed, punishes slow opponents, opponents can't develop their gameplan.
- **Weaknesses**: Runs out of cards quickly (floods out), board wipes are devastating, life gain stalls the game.
- **Examples**: Goblin decks, Mono-Red Burn, White Weenie, Zoo.
- **Sub-archetypes**: Hyper-aggro (all 1-drops), Stompy (few big efficient threats), Sligh (curve-based aggro).

### Control
**Goal**: Survive the early game, answer every threat, then win with a powerful late-game finisher or card advantage engine.
- **Key characteristics**: High removal density, counterspells, card draw, a few win conditions.
- **Win mechanism**: Attrition — outlast the opponent, then win with a 1-2 card threat when they're out of resources.
- **Strengths**: Flexible, adapts to any threat, wins very grindy games.
- **Weaknesses**: Slow, struggles vs fast aggro, needs to hit land drops, wrong answers for current threats.
- **Examples**: UW Control, Azorius Control, Esper Control, Dimir Control.
- **Playstyle**: Draw-go. Leave mana open. Act on opponent's end step.

### Midrange
**Goal**: Play efficient, individually powerful threats that out-value the opponent in a medium-length game.
- **Key characteristics**: Flexible threats that are good in multiple matchups, mix of interaction and proactive threats.
- **Win mechanism**: Out-value the opponent card by card with more efficient plays.
- **Strengths**: Good matchup spread, adaptable, plays well both as aggressor and defender.
- **Weaknesses**: Can be "fair" — loses to degenerate combo or hyper-fast aggro. Can be squeezed from both sides.
- **Examples**: Jund, Abzan, Sultai, Death's Shadow, Golgari Midrange.
- **Characteristics**: "The Goldilocks" archetype — not too fast, not too slow.

### Combo
**Goal**: Assemble a specific combination of cards that wins the game immediately or creates an insurmountable advantage.
- **Key characteristics**: Tutors, card draw, protection spells, redundant combo pieces.
- **Win mechanism**: The combo itself (infinite damage, infinite tokens, mill, etc.).
- **Strengths**: Can win out of nowhere, doesn't need to match opponent's gameplan.
- **Weaknesses**: Disruption (counterspells, discard, removal targeting pieces), needs to find pieces.
- **Examples**: Storm, Ad Nauseam, Reanimator, Splinter Twin, Scapeshift.
- **Sub-archetypes**: Turbo-combo (ignore everything, go as fast as possible), Toolbox combo (tutors to find answers and pieces), Resilient combo (multiple paths to victory).

### Tempo
**Goal**: Use efficient, cheap interaction to slow the opponent while applying fast pressure.
- **Key characteristics**: Cheap counterspells, cheap removal, efficient threats that apply pressure.
- **Win mechanism**: A creature that ends the game while cheap interaction keeps the opponent from responding.
- **Strengths**: Flexible, can disrupt combo and race aggro.
- **Weaknesses**: Needs to attack on multiple fronts simultaneously, mana-intensive.
- **Examples**: Delver (Izzet Delver, Temur Delver), Faeries, Merfolk.

## Commander-Specific Archetypes

### Voltron
**Goal**: Make one creature (usually the commander) very large and evasive enough to kill opponents with 21 commander damage.
- **Win mechanism**: 21 commander damage kills a player.
- **Examples**: Sram Equipment, Zurgo Helmsmasher, Rograkh/Ardenn.

### Stax/Prison
**Goal**: Tax or restrict opponents' ability to take actions, slowly grinding out a win.
- **Key terms**: Stax = cards with "each player sacrifices a permanent each upkeep" type effects. Prison = preventing combat, spells, etc.
- **Examples**: Hokori, Dust Drinker; Derevi tap/untap; Armageddon strategies.

### Aristocrats
**Goal**: Use a sacrifice engine to drain opponents through repeated death triggers.
- **Win mechanism**: Drain 1 life per creature death x many deaths = game. Or Blood Artist triggers.
- **Key pieces**: Sacrifice outlet + creatures that die + payoff for dying.
- **Examples**: Zulaport Cutthroat, Blood Artist, Dictate of Erebos.

### Tokens
**Goal**: Create many tokens and win through sheer numbers or pumping them all simultaneously.
- **Win mechanism**: Go-wide + Craterhoof Behemoth or overrun effect.
- **Examples**: Rhys the Redeemed, Thalisse, Elspeth planeswalkers.

### Superfriends
**Goal**: Accumulate many planeswalkers and generate insurmountable advantage through loyalty abilities.
- **Key pieces**: Proliferate effects to accelerate loyalty, Doubling Season for instant ultimates.

### Group Hug
**Goal**: Give everyone resources, advance the game state, usually with an alternate win condition.

### Spellslinger
**Goal**: Cast many instants and sorceries to trigger spell-based payoffs.
- **Win mechanism**: Guttersnipe-type effects, Thousand-Year Storm, Grapeshot storm.

## Draft Archetypes (General Patterns)

### Aggro in Draft
- Take efficient cheap creatures and combat tricks early.
- Prioritize evasion and direct damage (removal that doubles as reach).
- Watch for signaling: if 2-drops in your color are late, color is open.

### Control in Draft
- Prioritize unconditional removal and card draw.
- Take finishers (bombs) when they appear.
- Don't overload on answers — still need threats.

### Synergy/Tribal in Draft
- Commit to a tribe or mechanic once you see support pieces late (signals the archetype is open).
- Payoffs are more valuable the more enablers you have.

### Classic Color Pair Archetypes (general patterns seen across sets)
- **WU**: Tempo/Flyers — use cheap creatures with flying and cheap interaction.
- **WB**: Lifegain/Tokens/Aristocrats.
- **WR**: Aggro/Tokens with pump.
- **WG**: Counters/Go-wide.
- **UB**: Control/Mill/Graveyard.
- **UR**: Spellslinger/Tempo.
- **UG**: Ramp/Big creatures/Counters.
- **BR**: Sacrifice/Aggro.
- **BG**: Graveyard/Midrange/Counters.
- **RG**: Aggro/Ramp/Big Creatures.

## Interaction Archetypes (Cross-Strategy Concepts)

### Goodstuff
A deck without a specific synergy plan that just plays the most individually powerful cards. Relies on card quality over strategy.

### Toolbox
A deck with many silver bullets and tutors to find the right answer for any situation. Common in combo decks and midrange.

### Pod/Birthing Pod Style
Sacrifice creatures of one CMC to find creatures of CMC+1, generating incremental value up a chain.

### Reanimator
Discard or mill large creatures, then reanimate them cheaply. Bypasses the casting cost of expensive threats.

### Dredge
Use the dredge mechanic to fill the graveyard, then leverage graveyard cards as resources.

### Storm
Count the number of spells cast in a turn. Cast cheap cantrips and rituals to build storm count, then win with a storm spell (copies itself per storm count).

### Infect
Deal damage as -1/-1 counters to creatures and poison to players. 10 poison counters = player loses. Requires only 10 "damage" instead of 20 life.

### Landfall
Trigger abilities every time a land enters the battlefield. Combine with fetch lands (shuffle-find lands) and land ramping for maximum triggers.
