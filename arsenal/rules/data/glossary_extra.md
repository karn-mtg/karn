# MTG Community Glossary

## Card Evaluation Terms
- **Bomb**: A card so powerful it can win the game or generate overwhelming advantage on its own. In limited, a bomb is a rare/mythic that demands an immediate answer.
- **Removal**: A spell or ability that destroys, exiles, bounces, or otherwise neutralizes an opponent's permanent. The gold standard is unconditional removal (kills anything, no restrictions).
- **Unconditional removal**: Works on any target (e.g., "destroy target creature" with no other restriction).
- **Conditional removal**: Has restrictions (e.g., "destroy target non-black creature," "deal 3 damage to target creature").
- **Hard removal**: Permanently removes a permanent (destroy, exile).
- **Soft removal**: Temporarily neutralizes (tap, bounce, shrink).
- **Tempo removal**: Bouncing or tapping to gain time, not permanently solve the threat.
- **Answer**: Any card that deals with an opponent's threat.
- **Catch-all**: A card that answers many different types of threats (e.g., "exile target nonland permanent").
- **Silver bullet**: A specific sideboard card that decimates a particular strategy.
- **Hate card**: A card that specifically hates on a strategy (graveyard hate, artifact hate, etc.).

## Strategy Terms
- **Payoff**: A card that rewards you for executing a strategy. Example: "Whenever a goblin enters the battlefield, deal 1 damage to each opponent" is a payoff for a goblin strategy.
- **Enabler**: A card that makes a strategy possible. Example: a discard outlet enables madness or graveyard strategies.
- **Win condition (Win con)**: The card, combination, or plan that actually ends the game. Can be a combo, a creature that attacks for lethal, a burn spell, etc.
- **Alternate win condition (Alt win con)**: A non-standard way to win (e.g., Thassa's Oracle + empty library).
- **Engine**: A repeatable source of value. Usually involves multiple cards working together. Example: "Sacrifice a creature, draw a card" on a loop.
- **Value engine**: Generates card advantage or resources over time without necessarily winning immediately.
- **Draw engine**: Cards that generate consistent card draw.
- **Mana engine**: Cards that generate more mana than they cost (ramp, rituals, rocks).

## Card Advantage
- **Card advantage (CA)**: Having more cards or resources than your opponent.
- **2-for-1**: Getting two cards' worth of value from one card. Example: a sorcery that draws two cards.
- **Cantrip**: A spell that replaces itself by drawing a card. Effectively "free" card since you don't lose card advantage.
- **Looting**: Draw a card, then discard a card. Net-neutral but lets you filter.
- **Rummaging**: Discard a card, then draw a card. Same as looting but discard first.
- **Cycling**: Pay a cost to discard and draw, filtering your hand.
- **Wheels**: Draw 7 cards, often forcing opponent to do so too (named after Wheel of Fortune).
- **Impulse draw**: Exile top N cards, play one until end of turn. "Pseudo-draw."
- **Card selection**: Arranging which cards you'll draw (scry, surveil) without increasing total cards.
- **Scry N**: Look at top N cards, put any number on bottom, rest on top in any order.
- **Surveil N**: Look at top N cards, put any number in graveyard, rest on top. Better for graveyard strategies.
- **Brainstorm effect**: Draw 3, put 2 back on top. Classic card selection spell.

## Mana Terms
- **Ramp**: Generating more mana than your land drops allow. Usually refers to spells that put extra lands on the battlefield or create mana artifacts.
- **Mana rock**: An artifact that produces mana (e.g., Sol Ring, Arcane Signet).
- **Mana dork**: A creature that taps to produce mana (e.g., Llanowar Elves, Birds of Paradise).
- **Ritual**: A spell that generates more mana than it costs in a single burst (e.g., Dark Ritual gives 3B for 1B).
- **Mana sink**: A card you can pump excess mana into (e.g., X spells, activated pump abilities).
- **Fixing**: Cards that help you produce the right colors of mana.
- **Floated mana**: Mana held in your mana pool, not yet spent.
- **Tap out**: Using all your available mana, leaving you unable to respond to opponent's actions.
- **Open mana**: Leaving mana available to respond on opponent's turn.
- **Going off**: Successfully executing your combo or game-winning sequence.

## Tempo and Speed
- **Tempo**: Efficiency of plays relative to the game clock. A tempo play uses mana efficiently to advance your plan while slowing the opponent.
- **On-curve**: Playing a spell that uses exactly the mana available that turn (e.g., 2-drop on turn 2, 3-drop on turn 3).
- **Ahead on tempo**: Your plays are more efficient than your opponent's at a given point.
- **Behind on tempo**: Opponent is ahead — you're spending your turn reacting rather than developing your board.
- **Clock**: How many turns until a player wins at the current damage pace. "What's your clock?" = how fast can you kill?
- **Race**: Both players ignoring defense and trying to kill the other player faster.
- **Pressure**: Threatening the opponent's life total, forcing them to react defensively.
- **Goldfish**: Playing as if opponent doesn't exist. Used to test your kill speed.

## Board State Terms
- **Board wipe / Wrath**: A spell that destroys all creatures (or all permanents). Named after Wrath of God.
- **One-sided board wipe**: Destroys all opponent's creatures but not yours.
- **Flood**: Having too many lands, not enough spells.
- **Screw**: Not drawing enough lands (mana screw).
- **Blank**: A card that does nothing in the current game context.
- **Dead card**: A card in your hand with no useful target or application right now.
- **Live card**: A card that is relevant in the current game state.
- **Topdeck**: Drawing a card right off the top when you need it.
- **Hellbent**: Having no cards in hand.
- **Empty the hand**: Getting all cards out of your hand quickly (aggressive strategy).
- **Brick**: Draw a useless card.
- **Peel**: Draw specifically what you needed.

## Combat Terms
- **Alpha strike**: Attacking with all creatures, going for lethal.
- **Swing out**: Attack with everything.
- **Crack back**: Counter-attack after being attacked.
- **Chump block**: Sacrificing a small creature to block a large one to avoid taking damage.
- **Trade**: Both creatures die in combat (1-for-1).
- **Two-for-one**: One of your creatures kills two of opponent's creatures (through deathtouch, combat tricks, etc.).
- **Stonewalled**: A creature is too large to attack into profitably (opponent's blocker is too strong).
- **Evasion**: A keyword or ability that makes a creature hard to block (flying, trample, menace, etc.).
- **Evasive threat**: A creature that's hard to block.
- **Combat trick**: An instant that pumps your creature or shrinks an opponent's during combat.
- **Blowout**: A combat trick or instant that creates a huge one-sided advantage.
- **Brick wall**: A large defensive creature that stops opponent's attacks.

## Deck Composition
- **Curve**: The mana cost distribution of your deck.
- **Low to the ground**: A deck with many cheap spells and a low average mana cost.
- **Top-heavy**: A deck with many high-cost spells.
- **Gas**: Spells that generate value, threats, or card advantage. "I have gas" = I have good cards to play.
- **Threat density**: How many cards are actual threats vs answers/utility.
- **Air**: Cards that don't do enough. "That deck is full of air" = low-power cards.
- **Land base**: The lands in a deck and how they're configured.
- **Mana base**: The land and mana-producing cards in a deck.
- **Splash**: Adding a small number of cards in a third color with limited mana support.
- **Greedy mana base**: Attempting to support too many colors or expensive spells without proper fixing.

## Graveyard Terms
- **Mill**: Put cards from the top of the library into the graveyard. Can be offensive (milling opponent) or self-mill (enabling your own graveyard strategies).
- **Self-mill**: Intentionally milling yourself to fuel graveyard strategies.
- **Reanimate**: Return a card from the graveyard to the battlefield, often a large creature that was discarded or milled.
- **Graveyard recursion**: Any effect that returns cards from the graveyard.
- **Graveyard hate**: Cards that exile graveyards or prevent graveyard interactions.
- **Flashback**: Cast a spell from your graveyard (also a keyword mechanic).
- **Dumping**: Intentionally discarding cards into the graveyard.
- **Fueling the yard**: Actions that put cards in the graveyard for graveyard strategies.

## Combo Terminology
- **Combo**: A combination of two or more cards that creates a powerful, often game-winning effect.
- **Infinite combo**: A loop that can repeat an unlimited number of times (infinite mana, infinite damage, infinite tokens, infinite life, etc.).
- **Soft combo**: Two cards that work well together but aren't a game-ending infinite loop.
- **Hard combo**: A combination that wins the game immediately when assembled.
- **Tutor**: Search your library for a specific card.
- **Combo piece**: One of the cards required for a combo.
- **Missing piece**: The last combo piece you need to assemble the combo.
- **Lines**: A sequence of plays leading to a specific outcome. "What are your lines to win?" = what sequences can win you the game?
- **Loop**: A sequence of actions that can be repeated indefinitely.
- **Broken**: Extremely overpowered. Usually banned or restricted.
- **Degenerate**: A strategy that bypasses normal game rules in an unfun or overpowered way.
- **Prison**: Locking opponents out of taking normal game actions.
- **Lock**: A state where an opponent can't meaningfully interact with the game.

## Game Actions
- **Blink / Flicker**: Exile a permanent and return it to the battlefield. Resets ETB effects, removes counters, resets damage, detaches auras.
- **Bounce**: Return a permanent to its owner's hand.
- **Tuck**: Put a card on the bottom of its owner's library (or shuffle it in).
- **Exile**: Remove from game. Usually inaccessible without a specific card referencing it.
- **Counter**: Prevent a spell or ability from resolving (counter a spell on the stack).
- **Fog**: Prevent combat damage this turn.
- **Draw-go**: A control playstyle where you leave up mana every turn to respond and only act on opponent's end step.
- **Pass**: Pass priority / end your turn without acting.
- **Snap off**: Cast a spell immediately, usually reactively.
- **Hold up**: Keep mana available for an instant-speed response.

## Format-Specific
- **Meta**: The most popular/dominant decks in a format at a given time.
- **Tier 1**: A deck powerful enough to win major events consistently.
- **Tier 2**: Strong decks but not at the very top of the meta.
- **Brew**: A non-standard, creative deck that isn't part of the established meta.
- **Jank**: A low-power or highly inconsistent deck, often played for fun.
- **Goodstuff**: A deck built with the most individually powerful cards without a specific synergy plan.
- **Pile**: A loosely assembled deck without a clear strategy. Also "piles of cards."
- **Hedge**: A sideboard card that's generically useful across many matchups.
- **Sideboard (SB)**: 15 cards available to swap in/out between games in a match.
- **Transformational sideboard**: Sideboarding into a completely different deck strategy between games.
- **Signal**: In draft, passing an important card of a color signals to the next player that color is open.
- **Reading signals**: Understanding what colors/archetypes are available based on what's being passed.
- **Wheeling**: A card that goes all the way around the draft table back to you (very late pick signal).
- **P1P1**: Pack 1, Pick 1 — the very first pick of a draft. Usually signals your deck's direction.
